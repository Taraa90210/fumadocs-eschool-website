### **1. Fill in Subject Data per Class**

The first step is to ensure that all subjects have been entered according to the needs of each class.

- Access the "Subjects" menu on the left sidebar.
- Select the subjects to be taught in that class, both core subjects and elective subjects.
- Make sure all subjects are listed completely so they can be used during the schedule creation process.

**Note**: Filling in subject data is mandatory before the next steps can proceed smoothly.

---

### **2. Fill in Subject Teacher and Homeroom Teacher Data**

After the subject data is filled in, proceed with filling in the teacher data who teaches each subject and the homeroom teachers.

- Access the "Class Teachers" menu on the sidebar.
- Determine the teaching teacher for each subject that has been entered previously.
- Also determine the homeroom teacher for each existing study group.

**Attention**: Make sure the teacher data is correct so that the system can display teacher information accurately in the lesson schedule.

---

### **3. Arrange Lesson Schedule (Drag and Drop)**

After all subject and teacher data is completely filled in, the lesson schedule creation process can be done through the "Create Schedule" menu located in the "Class Schedule" menu.

- You can arrange the schedule by dragging and dropping subjects to the appropriate day and time columns.
- Arrange the schedule by paying attention to time allocation, break times, and lesson distribution to be balanced.
- Make sure there is no overlap between teachers and the subjects they teach.

---

### **Conclusion**

To create an effective lesson schedule, follow these steps:

- Ensure **Subject** data has been filled in.
- Continue with filling in **Class Teachers** to determine teachers and homeroom teachers.
- Arrange schedules with the **drag and drop** feature in the Create Schedule menu.
- Finish by pressing **Save** so the schedule data is updated in the system.

By following the steps above, the lesson schedule creation process will run effectively and efficiently, and minimize data errors.
