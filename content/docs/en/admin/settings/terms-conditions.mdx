The **Terms & Conditions** page is an important part of the **E-SCHOOL SIAKAD Plus** system that serves to provide official information regarding regulations, limitations, and terms of service use.\
The content on this page typically includes legal arrangements, definitions of terms, user rights and obligations, and platform usage policies.

The main goal is to ensure that all parties using the system understand and agree to the applicable rules before utilizing the service.

***

#### **Functions and Benefits**

1. **Legal Protection** – Provides a legal basis for the school and the service provider in regulating interactions with users.
2. **Clarity of Definitions** – Explains the meaning of important terms such as *User*, *Service*, and *Content* to avoid misunderstanding.
3. **Service Transparency** – Outlines rights, obligations, and service limitations clearly so that users know the scope of use.
4. **Compliance** – Ensures that all parties accessing the service have read and agreed to the applicable regulations.

***

#### **Page Access**

* **Menu Location**:\
  Navigate to **Settings → Terms & Conditions** in the sidebar.
* **Public URL**:\
  Displayed at the top of the page so it can be shared with relevant parties, such as students, teachers, or parents.
* **Content Editor**:\
  Uses a *Rich Text Editor* to facilitate writing, text formatting, and adding elements such as links, tables, or images.

***

#### **Usage Guide**

1. **Reading Content**
   * Ensure that the entire content of the Terms & Conditions complies with school policy and applicable regulations.
2. **Editing Content**
   * Use the editor to update the text as needed, such as policy revisions or service adjustments.
3. **Saving Changes**
   * Click the **Save** button at the bottom right to ensure changes are saved and displayed on the public URL.
4. **Policy Alignment**
   * Before publishing, coordinate with the school or management to ensure the content is appropriate and legally valid.

***

#### **Important Notes**

* Use formal, clear, and unambiguous language.
* Avoid using technical terms that are difficult to understand without explanation.
* Perform regular updates to adapt to developments in school policy or government regulations.
* Major changes should be accompanied by official notification to users.
